Urban Data Science for Social Good

The Data Science for Social Good (DSSG) summer program brings together data and domain scientists to work on focused, collaborative projects that are designed to impact public policy for societal benefit.

Modeled after similar programs at the University of Chicago and Georgia Tech, with elements from the University of Washington's eScience Institute's Data Science Incubator, DSSG Student Fellows are selected to work with academic researchers, data scientists, and public stakeholder groups on data-intensive research projects.

This year both the University of Washington and the University of British Columbia hosted independent DSSG programs, with midterm presentations made at the first CUAC Summit at UBC on July 13-14, 2017. This culminated in a cross-university Fall Symposium at UW to share in experiences, research and lessons learned September 11-12, 2017.

Through partnerships with nonprofit organizations and government agencies, the 2017 DSSG teams completed projects examining electronic fare card data, transit network patterns, traffic cruising, early childhood education, tourism asset utilization, neighborhood equity and economic investment opportunities. Teams partnered with the City of Surrey, a growing region in the Vancouver metropolitan area, and with British Columbia, United Way, and the Seattle Department of Transportation. They created multi-layered data visualizations and interactive portals showing neighborhood-level information for city planners and the public.
